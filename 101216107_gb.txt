George Brown College was chartered in 1966 and opened in 1967, with the formation of Ontario's community college system.
The school was named after a 19th century politician and newpaper publisher, who found the Toronto Globe, now named The Globe and Mail.
The George Brown College of Applied Arts and Technology, commonly known as George Brown Polytechnic since 2025, is a publicly funded college of applied arts and technology with three campuses located in downtown Toronto, Ontario, Canada.
